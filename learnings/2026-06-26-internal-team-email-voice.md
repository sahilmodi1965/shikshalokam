# Internal team-announcement email voice — what Sonal's edits taught the brain

The brain drafted a team-wide announcement (Mantra + ShikshaLokam) introducing the SL Brain.
Sonal edited the draft in Gmail; the brain read it back via the new `gs.py draft-read` and
diffed intent vs. final. Three durable signals for **internal team comms** (distinct from
external stakeholder/social voice):

1. **Open warmer and more relational.** "Hi all," → **"Dear team,"**, and add a check-in line —
   **"Hope you are doing well."** (Echoes the canonical newsletter opener "Hope this email finds
   you in good spirits" — the warm check-in carries into internal mail too.)
2. **Name the thing plainly up front.** Sonal added an explicit framing line:
   *"we've been bringing AI into our work to build the efficiency of the team."* For an internal
   announcement she wants the literal **what** (AI, team efficiency) stated, not only the softer
   "a new way of working." Lead with the concrete capability, then the brand name.
3. **Soften clever punchiness.** "Quick one — and an exciting one." → *"Here's a quick update, an
   exciting one at that."* The em-dash one-liner reads as a touch performative for team mail;
   she prefers a plainer, friendlier cadence.

Unchanged (the brain got these right): the **"What we've automated so far"** bulleted pointers,
the "single source of truth" framing, and the "it gets smarter every time one of us uses it" close.

**How to apply:** for any internal team announcement from Sonal, open "Dear team," + a one-line
check-in; state the concrete capability plainly before the brand name; keep cadence friendly over
clever; keep tight scannable bullets for the "what changed" block.
