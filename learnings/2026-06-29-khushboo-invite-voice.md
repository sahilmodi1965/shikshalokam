# Khushboo invite voice — what Sonal's direct edits taught the brain

Sonal hand-edited the Arundhati Bhattacharya InvokED 6.0 invite (Khushboo's name) in the register
doc; read back via `gs.py doc-read`. Three durable signals for InvokED invite drafts:

1. **Honorific for senior leaders.** "Dear Arundhati," → **"Dear Arundhati ma'am,"**. For senior /
   distinguished invitees, use a respectful salutation ("ma'am" / "sir", alongside the existing
   Shri / Smt. / Dr. / Ji conventions). Don't address them by first name alone.
2. **Cleaner punctuation; smoother sentences.** She removed the dash and several commas and merged
   choppy clauses: "It was such a pleasure to meet you. Our conversation has stayed with me ever
   since- and your warmth, and your generous offer..." → "It was such a pleasure to meet you, and
   our conversation has stayed with me ever since. Your warmth and your generous offer... meant more
   to me and to all of us than I can easily put into words." Prefer flowing sentences over
   comma/dash-stacked clauses. (Reinforces the styleguide's "fewer em-dashes, vary sentence length.")
3. **Don't over-explain the movement.** She cut "Shikshagraha is our growing movement to make quality
   education a reality for every child in India- and". For a recipient who already knows ShikshaLokam,
   skip the promotional movement definition and go straight to the substance (InvokED, the invite).

**How to apply:** in invite drafts, open with the right honorific for the person's standing; write in
clean flowing sentences (sparing commas/dashes); and trust the reader's context — don't pad with a
Shikshagraha mission gloss when they already know us.
