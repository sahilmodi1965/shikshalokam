# Nagaland Coffee Book — stories in progress

A working pool of stories for ShikshaLokam's Nagaland coffee-table book. Stories begin as `wip`, move to `review-ready` when Sonal flags them, and `locked` once final. Each story is one `### Story — <Title>` section. The HTML mirror at `docs/projects/nagaland-coffee-book.html` carries the same shape with copy-friendly titles.

## Project intent

The Nagaland coffee book sits inside ShikshaLokam's broader Small Steps / Micro Miracles series — visual-first volumes that carry **stories of teachers, ed-leaders, communities, and students** who moved one specific outcome in one specific place. Distinct from the Brazil presentation (slide format) and the SLC blogs (long-form first-person) — coffee books carry **short, image-paired vignettes** (≤300 words per story).

**Canonical container (as of 2026-05-26):** **Nagaland Literacy & Numeracy Fest (NLNF) 3.0** — the
state's drive towards **NIPUN Nagaland (foundational literacy and numeracy for all by 2027).** This
book celebrates **15 NLNF 3.0 winning schools** under two umbrella themes Sonal named in her brief:
**collective effort** and **micro-improvement (series of small steps to achieve an objective).**

NLNF 3.0 structure (see [[../wiki/concepts/programs]] § NLNF):

- **Pre-Primary → Grade 3** — five MIP cycles around foundational skills.
- **Grades 4 and 5** — Project-Based Learning in language and mathematics (this is the strand the
  **DEP / Nagaland PBL** macro story sits inside — 400 teachers, 200 schools across Dimapur and
  Kiphire; see [[shikshalokam-brazil-stories-may2026]] § Nagaland).
- Structured mentoring · Mega PTMs · school/district/state recognition stack · governance + evaluation.

**The collective behind NLNF 3.0:** Department of School Education + Samagra Shiksha Nagaland + SCERT
Nagaland · ShikshaLokam (project management, micro-improvements design, government liaison,
technology) · Mantra4Change · NagaED · Education Above All (content + funding).

## Stories

### Story — Pebbles, poems, and a borrowed television

- **Status:** `review-ready`
- **Subject:** A four-teacher cohort led by **Head Teacher Kuzie Kulim** — Ichaulu (Literacy),
  Rebiheing (Numeracy), Maheingaule (Pre-Primary).
- **Location:** **Government Middle School Jalukie Sector-B, Peren district, Nagaland.**
- **Lead image:** *Pending — four teacher profile photos + two school exteriors + eight project-implementation photos all consented and uploaded to Drive (see [[nlnf3-gms-jalukie-sectorb-response-2026-05-07]] § Image assets). Editorial pick TBD.*
- **Title (working):** **Pebbles, poems, and a borrowed television.**
- **Subtitle (working):** *How GMS Jalukie Sector-B — one of fifteen NLNF 3.0 winning schools — built
  foundational learning out of what was already in the village.*
- **Hook (1 line):** In a school where the network drops, the power flickers, and most parents are
  daily-wage earners, foundational learning began the day the Head Teacher carried in a television.

- **Body (~290 words):**

  In **Peren district**, the road between schools is hilly, the network is uncertain, and the
  electricity is not promised. At **Government Middle School Jalukie Sector-B**, most students are
  the first in their families to attend school. When the rains come, attendance falls — children
  are needed at home, on the land, with the cattle. This is the school that, in 2026, won its way
  into the fifteen.

  The Fest asked them to do things they had never done. Record videos. Upload them through a network
  that wouldn't hold. *"Uploading project videos often took a long time,"* the school writes. *"Despite
  these difficulties, we tried our best to complete and submit all project work on time."* When the
  pre-primary classroom needed audio-visual learning — and the school had no television — Head Teacher
  **Kuzie Kulim** arranged one *"so that students studying in government schools would not feel
  disadvantaged compared to others."*

  Then the small steps began.

  Literacy teacher **Ichaulu** introduced a poem and read it aloud, line by line. Children recited
  with her — with actions, with expression — every week from Term 1 onwards. Numeracy teacher
  **Rebiheing** picked up the everyday objects of the classroom — and asked children to arrange them
  by size, by weight, by number. Heavy and light. Heaviest and lightest. Pre-primary teacher
  **Maheingaule** opened her bag to what the village already had: *pebbles, twigs, leaves, building
  blocks, visuals.* Children, she writes, are *"more enthusiastic and eager to learn compared to
  conventional method."*

  Parents — many of whom had never been to school themselves — were uneasy at first. The school
  called Parent-Teacher Meetings, explained the purpose, and the unease became participation.

- **What changed:** *"Quality foundational learning is possible even with limited resources."* — the
  school's own line. Reading, pronunciation, vocabulary, comprehension all up in the literacy class;
  size, weight, ordering — and confidence — up in numeracy; communication, social interaction,
  creativity, and joy up in pre-primary. Class participation went from quiet to active across all three.

- **Their own forward-looking line:**
  > *"With creativity, teamwork and community support, students can become active, confident and enthusiastic learners."*

- **What they will keep doing, after the Fest:**
  Activity-based learning · play-based learning · storytelling · poem recitation with actions ·
  locally-available teaching-learning materials · regular Parent-Teacher Meetings. *"Because they
  have helped students become more active, confident, creative and enthusiastic learners."*

- **The collective behind this story:**
  - **Department of School Education, Nagaland** + **Samagra Shiksha Nagaland** + **SCERT Nagaland** —
    co-design, recognition, scale-out
  - **ShikshaLokam** — project management, micro-improvements design, government liaison, technology
  - **Mantra4Change** — capacity building for education leaders
  - **NagaED** — contextual programme design and on-ground implementation
  - **Education Above All** — content design and funding
  - **The school's own four-teacher cohort + the parents and community of Jalukie Sector-B** — who
    showed up, sceptical at first, partners by the end

- **Attribution / consent:** **Written consent — agreed.** The school's Head Teacher marked *I agree*
  to *"the Department of School Education, Samagra Shiksha Nagaland, SCERT Nagaland, and ShikshaLokam
  using our responses, quotes, and photographs in the NLNF 3.0 Coffee Table Book and related
  communications."* Image assets uploaded to Drive — links in [[nlnf3-gms-jalukie-sectorb-response-2026-05-07]] § Image assets.
- **Source thread:** Form response submitted **7 May 2026, 13:52 IST** — ingested as
  [[nlnf3-gms-jalukie-sectorb-response-2026-05-07]]. Routed to brain on **26 May 2026** via Sonal's

#### Voice notes — what this story kept

- *Story-first, data after.* The reader meets Peren, the borrowed television, and Kuzie Kulim before
  meeting *micro-improvement* as a term. The term arrives the way it earns itself — as a series of
  small, named acts (poem, pebbles, PTM).
- *Named everyone.* All four teachers by name; the school by name; the district by name; every
  partner CSO + the government bodies by name + role. Per the Brazil-presentation collective-naming
  pattern ([[shikshalokam-brazil-stories-may2026]]) and the "Frame them as contributors, not content"
  caption rule.
- *Their own words for the close.* Not a strapline; the school's own one-line forward-looking note.
- *Numbers held to one place.* Where the source has them (15 winning schools, four teachers, five
  MIP cycles for Pre-Primary–G3), they appear. Where the source doesn't — student counts, attendance
  percentages — we don't invent. Coffee-book copy can be field-supplemented in the next pass.
