# Captions — rolling option pool

Caption candidates grouped by the weekday rhythm Sonal authored in `routes/captions.md`. Each slot can carry 2–3 variants. Status moves `draft → approved → posted`. Posted captions archive weekly to `projects/captions/posted/<YYYY-MM-DD>.md`.

## How this page works

- Sonal in chat: *"draft Tuesday's research insight post about [topic]"* → Claude drops 2–3 variants under **Tuesday — Tech / Research Insight**, each with platform tag (`LinkedIn` / `X`) and `status: draft`.
- Sonal copies the title (first line) of the variant she wants back into chat → status flips to `approved`. Voice rule that landed is noted under the variant.
- Sonal flags posted → status flips to `posted`, archives at end of week.
- "Not my voice" / "rewrite" on any variant → 2–3 fresh variants in next turn, structural feedback routes to `learnings/`.

### Variant 2 — "Reform begins with curriculum. Shikshagraha begins with the leader."

- **Platform:** LinkedIn
- **Status:** `draft`
- **Theme:** agency for education leaders, anchored on OECD research consensus
- **Sources cited:** OECD *Improving School Leadership* programme (Pont, Nusche & Moorman, 2008) and the broader body of OECD work — **paraphrased, not quoted** (Sonal to confirm exact citation before posting)
- **Voice anchor:** pulled-finding opener → short-line cadence → on-team "we" → I HAVE / I CAN / I WISH framework from [[shikshalokam-2-strategy-note-jul2025]]

> Across two decades of OECD research, one finding has held:
> the school leader is second only to the teacher in shaping student outcomes.
>
> Yet most reform begins with the curriculum.
>
> Shikshagraha begins with the leader.
>
> The shift from compliance to agency.
> From *"what's allowed?"* to *"what's possible?"*
>
> Three sentences our 570,000+ education leaders carry into their schools:
> **I HAVE. I CAN. I WISH.**

> Share your thoughts in the comments below 👇

> #InvokED2026 #SchoolLeadership #CollectiveAction
