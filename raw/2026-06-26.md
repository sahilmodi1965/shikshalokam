**The Hidden Crisis in Indian Education**
India's 1.5 lakh schools possess untapped solutions that remain inaccessible to those who need them.
Two examples: in Sheohar district, Bihar, a girl previously destined for marriage now studies maths
and science through Shiksha Chaupal's work; in Haryana, the 'Aao School Chalein' initiative re-enrolled
dropout students through community-led door-to-door campaigns. Teachers nationwide implement
innovative solutions — from seating arrangements to attendance strategies — yet "these breakthroughs
remain invisible or concentrated where they were born." The piece asks: "Doesn't it feel like having
treasure scattered across the country, but no map to find it?"

**Why Ground Stories Are Non-Negotiable**
Improvement discussions typically centre on experts and policymakers, but "the discussion is still
incomplete" without voices from teachers, school leaders, and communities — insights policy papers
cannot capture. Excluding them means "taking decisions based on incomplete information."

**The Barriers Keeping These Stories Silent**
These narratives rarely surface due to "practical and psychological" obstacles (not elaborated in
Part 1).

**Meeting the Moment With A Listening Tool**
The solution: a tool that "feels like talking to a friend, not filling out a never-ending
questionnaire." Introduced as **MItra** — enabling listening, documentation, and sharing of stories
"at scale."

**What's Next**
Part 2 will explore MItra's development and design philosophy.
