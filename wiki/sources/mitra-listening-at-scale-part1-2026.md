# Listening at Scale (Part 1 of 3): Why It Matters

ShikshaLokam's published blog introducing **MItra**, the team's voice-enabled listening tool. First
of a three-part **"Stories That Matter" / "Listening at Scale"** series. Published **25 June 2026**.
Co-authored by **Ayush Tank, Munna Devanand Goupale, Niveditha Mohan, Prateek Agarwal, Sonal Bhasin,
Syed Hyder Ali, Vinaya Ashok Kurtkoti, Yogita Tambulkar**. Shared into the brain by Ayush (co-author),

## What the piece says (faithful summary)

- **The hidden crisis.** India's **1.5 lakh schools** hold untapped solutions that don't reach those
  who need them. Real examples: a girl in **Sheohar district, Bihar**, once destined for marriage, now
  studies maths and science through Shiksha Chaupal's work; in **Haryana**, the **'Aao School Chalein'**
  initiative re-enrolled dropouts via community-led door-to-door campaigns. Teachers everywhere invent
  fixes — seating, attendance — yet "these breakthroughs remain invisible or concentrated where they
  were born."
- **The image (reusable):** *"Doesn't it feel like having treasure scattered across the country, but
  no map to find it?"*
- **Why ground stories are non-negotiable.** Improvement talk centres experts and policymakers, but
  "the discussion is still incomplete" without teachers, school leaders, and communities. Leaving them
  out means "taking decisions based on incomplete information."
- **The barriers.** These stories rarely surface because of "practical and psychological" obstacles
  (Part 1 names them but doesn't elaborate — likely expanded later in the series).
- **The tool — MItra.** A listening tool that "feels like talking to a friend, not filling out a
  never-ending questionnaire" — letting leaders share micro-improvements in their own words, so stories
  can be listened to, documented, and shared **at scale**.

## What MItra is (for reuse in drafts)

MItra = ShikshaLokam's **voice-enabled listening tool** that lets school, community, and youth leaders
share their micro-improvements conversationally (in their own language), turning quiet ground-level
solutions into stories worth learning from. Framed as "an education leader's friend." Connects directly
to the Micro-Improvement movement (the [[programs]] frame) and the [[leadership]] thread.
