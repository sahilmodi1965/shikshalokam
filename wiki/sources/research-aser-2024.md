# Annual Status of Education Report (ASER) 2024

**Full citation:** ASER Centre / Pratham Education Foundation (2025). *Annual Status
of Education Report (Rural) 2024.* Released 28 January 2025.

**Trust tier:** Tier 1 — India's largest citizen-led household survey of children's
schooling and learning, running annually since 2005.

**Scale of the 2024 round:** 17,997 villages · 605 districts · 649,491 children.
**URL:** https://asercentre.org/
**Verified:** 2026-05-21 via the `shikshalokam-research` skill (WebSearch — confirmed
release date, survey scale, and the headline figures below).

## The canonical findings (cite verbatim — numbers exact)

**Foundational reading — the recovery:**
- Class 3 children **in government schools** who can read a Class 2-level text:
  **16.3% (2022) → 23.4% (2024)** — the **highest since ASER began in 2005.**
- Class 5 students reading at Class 2 level: **38.5% (2022) → 44.8% (2024).**

**Foundational numeracy:**
- Class 3 students who can do subtraction: **28.1% (2018) → 33.7% (2024).**

**Who led the recovery:**
- **Government schools surpassed private schools** in learning recovery —
  credited to NEP 2020 and the NIPUN Bharat Mission.

**The honest other half (always pair with the gains):**
- **76.6% of Class 3 children still cannot read a Class 2-level text.** The gap
  remains wide; the *direction* is what changed.

## SL-messaging angle

**Education equity — the hopeful-but-honest recovery story.** This is the freshest
hard data point for ShikshaLokam's narrative that public/government schools are
*places of hope, not problems to be solved*. The government-schools-led recovery is
the strongest available evidence that systemic, collective effort moves the
foundational-learning needle. Use it for equity messaging — always naming both the
gain and the gap, per the voice rule (hopeful but honest).
