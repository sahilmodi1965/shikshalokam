## Career arc (in his words / from his account)

- **Origin:** Village on the railway line between **Gudur and Renigunta** [transcript: "Gudor and Rigont"],
  now in **Tirupati district** (Andhra Pradesh) post-reorganisation. Village school under a tree, no walls.
- **First teacher:** *Viru Patnam Sankara Raju* [transcript: "Viru Patram Sankar Razu"] — 8th-standard-trained
  Higher Grade teacher who walked ~2 km daily from a village called "Pigilong." Was the village's teacher
  for the full 20–25 years from VNM's birth to his joining service. Personally appealed to VNM's father
  when VNM was about to drop out after 5th class.
- **Family:** Four sisters, one brother. *"Among them myself only struggled and studied; all other in
  primary and high school level dropped."* Father: low-level railway employee, semi-literate, but woke
  the household at 4:30 AM daily and made young Nagoor *"write 1, 2, 3, 4 like that, words and… railway
  station names, village names like that."* Mother: housewife.
- **Two dropouts.** First after 5th class — no local tradition of going to high school, nearest school
  3 km away, walking on live railway tracks across rail bridges *"between the two patalu [rails]."*
  Second after 7th class — only 11 of 55 students passed. Both times pulled back by an outsider:
  the primary teacher, then a *distant relative — a diploma engineer* who got him admitted to a
  **Scheduled Tribe hostel in Gudur** [transcript: "Gore"] for 9th and 10th. Hostel itself:
  *"horrible — old building, the different attitude, they don't allow to read books, they don't allow
  to keep books also."*
- **Study light:** kerosene wick stuffed through a cloth-plugged hole in a tonic-syrup bottle lid.
  Kerosene rationed — *"not allowed beyond seven or so"* — so most reading happened in daylight.
- **1979:** Passed 10th class in 2nd class. *"After almost 14–15 years of the establishment of the
  school, I am the first person to pass."*
- **Higher ed:** Intermediate MPC → BSc MPC (college topper in 2nd year, first-class degree). On the
  way to pay MSc fees, learnt of admission to the **Government Comprehensive College of Education
  (B.Ed)** — one of only three such colleges in undivided AP (Hyderabad-Masab Tank, Rajahmundry,
  Nellore) — and switched tracks mid-walk. In MSc 1st year, was selected (March 1986) as one of ~34
  *regular* teachers from a pool of 400–500 special-teacher applicants in the District Selection
  Committee recruitment. Chose the teaching post over completing MSc.

## Concrete moves (cite-ready)

### As teacher (1986–95)
- **Enrolment-led strategy.** *"On the basis of strength enrollment of the students, the other facilities
  will come."* Upgraded two government schools from upper-primary to high-school status — the second was a
  defunct upper primary a Mandal President personally asked him to revive after watching him turn the
  first around.
- Cleared **Group I Prelims (1991)**; topped general studies and general essay in Group I Mains (1992);
  selected as **Deputy Educational Officer in 1993** — *DEO is higher than Group I standard.*

### As DEO, Hyderabad zone (1993 onwards)
- **Reformed the shift system.** Moved most Hyderabad government schools from morning / afternoon shifts
  to a single **general shift** against heavy resistance from teacher groups. *"There's a lot of
  resistance from teacher groups… [I] never compromised."*
- **Pioneered counselling-based teacher transfers (1996 DSC).** Replaced patronage / pull-driven transfers
  with a transparent merit-and-seniority counselling system: published vacancies, open allocation. *"That
  has become a major change in administrative system — not only in education department, other department
  also nowadays counselling system is going on. That was originated in Hyderabad district."*
- Paired with **rationalisation of teachers.**

### As DEO, Medak / Nalgonda
- **Curbed exam malpractice at the micro level** — cleaned up conduct and evaluation. Result: a temporary
  drop in 10th-class pass percentages, then those same cohorts topped intermediate — *"that district was
  in the top. That quality was maintained."*
- Refused to suspend three school heads on the hierarchy's order after a zero-result year without
  investigating — defended two struggling principals who had personally fed and housed their 10th-class
  teachers to keep the school alive. The District Collector later publicly appreciated them.

### As Director, CSE / Samagra Shiksha AP (final ~5.5 years)
- **Co-led the project document for SALT — Supporting Andhra's Learning Transformation** (World
  Bank-supported). *"We are able to execute the project on time and able to get the World Bank support
  as expected by the government."*
- **PM SHRI scheme delivery:** secured **second-highest count of PM SHRI schools in India** for AP —
  after UP only — despite AP being the 6th-most-populous state. **Phase 1: 661 schools. Phase 2: 193
  schools. Phase 3: ~80 schools. Total ~935 schools.**
- **School Leadership Development Programme (SLDP).** Identified the missing piece in two-three decades
  of Indian education reform: *"a number of trainings to the teachers — teaching community pedagogy,
  curriculum, teacher trainings — but in some way, leaders were not trained and motivated properly."*
  Selected **250 school heads** on merit and deputed them to **IIM Ahmedabad for a 5-day residential
  leadership programme.** Cascaded under their guidance to **all school leaders across ~45,000 AP
  schools** (8-day residential for first cohort, 6-day at regional level). *"It impacted a lot. It
  reflected in the ISA / ISSY results and quality of education."*
- **SSC → CBSE transition** work — partial period.
- **Andhra Pradesh Residential Schools Society** (additional charge, final 1y 3m) — visited *"each and
  every corner of the institutions"* outside hours / on holidays, brought in alumni support to refurbish
  40-year-old infrastructure last strengthened in the 1980s under N. T. Rama Rao garu.

### As Director, Jawahar Bal Bhavan (~2 years)
- Inherited a moribund department of **222 centres** across AP with no annual state-level programming.
  **Restored two annual state-level 3-day residential programmes** for children. *"That was the last —
  there was no programmes earlier and there was no programmes after. The two years two annual programmes
  they remained as record."*

## Verbatim quotes (use as-is)

1. *"Discipline and hard work serves and achieves anything. That is my belief — blind belief from the childhood."*
2. *"A teacher can do anything. A teacher only can change the individual — in turn, society."*
3. *"In spite of so many positions, pleasures, [perks], hot deals — I never compromised in my 40 years of
   service, and never deviated at any level, even political pressure, official pressure."*
4. *"Rights arise from the responsibilities. If we perform some responsibilities, then we will get some
   rights. If you don't perform responsibility, then you don't have rights."*
5. *"It is not for me, for individual — it is for the position, it is for the seat. So many people are
   expecting a lot from you. If I do as for my whims and fancies — if the same thing happened with the
   authority who appointed me, I won't be here."*
6. *"The teaching profession is a noble profession. If anybody wishes to come to that profession, you must
   — you must — [come] for faith. Not with other motives. Not for employment. Not to get something. This
   is the manufacturing of future generations — holding the future generations in all respects."*
7. *"If we want to get something, we have to forgo something."*
8. *"That is the secret of my success — we have followed the seniors' expertise and experiences, practices,
   I try to adopt as maximum as possible and implement the same."*
9. *"Aims must be good. Aims must be good. If aims are good, results automatically will be good."*
   *(Repeated twice in the wrap — preferred closing line.)*
10. On the interviewer's framing of his arc as *"from teacher to commissioner"*, he cuts in: *"Not from
    the teacher — from dropout of fifth class."*
