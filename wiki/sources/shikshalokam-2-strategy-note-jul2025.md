# ShikshaLokam 2.0 Strategy Note — the SL-org positioning document

**Drive (Google Doc draft, owner neeraj@):** https://docs.google.com/document/d/1O3auDLtgbiunHr0h0vK-moLJIja-IkshNGWR9N_LiGI/edit

**Marked DRAFT in source — but this is the canonical SL-2.0 positioning artefact circulated since Jul 2025.**

**Why this matters:** the brain previously held the **Shikshagraha movement** narrative ([[shikshagraha-narrative-2026]]) but lacked the **ShikshaLokam-org** narrative. This note fills that gap. It is the foundational document explaining SL 1.0 → SL 2.0 and SL's role as **co-builder of Shikshagraha**.

## One-line positioning (verbatim)

> *"ShikshaLokam, established in 2017 by the Shibulal Family Philanthropic Initiatives (SFPI), is a mission that is focused on **Enabling leadership development across India's K-12 education system.**"*

## The SL 1.0 → 2.0 thesis

> *"ShikshaLokam is now evolving from being a platform to a network."*

| | SL 1.0 (2017–2024) | SL 2.0 (2025 →) |
|---|---|---|
| Form | Platform | Network |
| Play | Prototype platform play | Network play |
| Role | Build the leadership-development platform | Co-builder + system orchestrator + movement builder for **Shikshagraha** |
| Reach metric | "Relevant" (30%, 10%, 2.5% of users under intervention) | "Significant" → "Standardised" (80%, 60% target) |
| Mission target | Empower 4.5M education leaders by 2030 | Same target; achieved via the network not the platform |

## The 7-year SL 1.0 cumulative impact (verbatim from the note)

- **Leadership development embedded across 30% of public schools** (~over 30M school-going children).
- **600,000 micro-improvements** catalysed in 25+ states and UTs.
- **50+ nonprofit ecosystem partners** across 16 states.
- Approach **institutionalised at federal level** via Vidya Amrit + DIKSHA.
- Recognised by **Schwab, Ashoka, Catalyst 2030, Teach for All**.

> Note: the [[shikshagraha-brief-collaterals-feb2026]] source has **fresher current-cycle numbers** (31K+ MIPs, 15 states, 33 districts, 21 partners, 1.03L women). The 2.0 Strategy Note's numbers are **7-year cumulative SL totals.** Use accordingly — recency wins for "now" claims; cumulative wins for "since 2017" claims.

## SL 2.0 — the network play (verbatim summary)

> *"In this new phase, **ShikshaLokam 2.0** will take on the core role of a **movement builder**, particularly for Shikshagraha. To catalyse both national and global conversations around education leadership and systemic school improvements, it will require the involvement of diverse philanthropic institutions and key stakeholders from the outset."*

**Two SL 2.0 roles:**

1. **System Orchestrator** — *"facilitating meaningful and impactful collective action … developing scalable frameworks, platforms, and initiatives … aligned toward common goals, accelerating systemic change in the education sector."*
2. **Movement Builder** — *"storytelling, advocacy, and public engagement efforts that mobilise grassroots participation … creating a bottom-up narrative and engaging the public as active participants."*

## New capabilities SL 2.0 must add (verbatim list)

- Open Source Expertise
- Network Weaving
- Program Design (large-scale, public-system tailored)
- Evidence Creation (globally relevant research)
- Global Advocacy
- Fund Orchestration (HNIs + institutions + philanthropies)

## The 10 verbs of the movement (load-bearing)

The note defines **10 actions** anyone joining Shikshagraha contributes through. This is the canonical verb list for participation:

1. **Amplify** — spread the message
2. **Convene** — bring stakeholders together
3. **Implement** — execute programs in schools / communities
4. **Design** — innovative frameworks, tools, solutions
5. **Orchestrate** — coordinate resources / expertise / actions
6. **Enable** — provide support + capacity-building
7. **Connect** — establish partnerships
8. **Mentor** — guide emerging leaders
9. **Fund** — financial backing
10. **Build Narrative** — craft and share stories

## The ecosystem roles in Shikshagraha (verbatim category map)

- **Co-Builders** — shaping, championing, anchoring development in their geography. SL Foundation is the co-builder in India.
- **Strategic Partners** — long-term funding + tech + knowledge. *Named: Axis Bank, AWS, Societal Thinking.*
- **Anchor Partners** — sub-collective drivers; founders commit to being Weavers. *Named: Commutiny (youth leadership vertical).*
- **Collaborators** — paid specialised partners (academic / design / consulting). *Named: Gnothi Seauton (strategic comms), CoLab (program design + evaluation).*
- **Momentum Partners** — on-ground change initiatives. *Named: Vidhya Vidhai (Tamil Nadu), Jyothi Mahila Samakhya (Bihar women's federation).*
- **Ambassadors** — public figures bringing mainstream visibility.
- **Advisors** — strategic direction.
- **Mentors** — practitioners supporting Momentum Partners on the field.

## SL Foundation — internal team structure (verbatim)

ShikshaLokam Foundation (Section 8 non-profit; operational costs covered by SFPI) is organised internally as:

- **Weavers (Strategic Leadership Team)** — monthly meeting, long-term vision
- **Core Leadership Team** — daily operations
- **Center of Excellence (CoE — Knowledge, Research & Design)** — evidence + frameworks; partnered with global academic institutions
- **Funding Orchestration Team**
- **System Orchestration Team** — strategic government relationships
- **Ecosystem Development** — NGO + SHG onboarding
- **Narrative Building (Strategic Communications)** — storytelling, media, public awareness
- **Digital Public Goods (ELEVATE: Energised Leadership with Enhanced Visibility and Administrative Transformation towards Excellence)** — products & technology team

## Origin story — SFPI → NILE → ShikshaLokam (verbatim)

> The Shibulal family began its philanthropic journey in 1998 with a scholarship scheme in S.D. Shibulal's hometown in Kerala. SFPI now spans education, healthcare, organic farming, social welfare.
>
> The leadership-development intent was first realised as **NILE (National Institute for Leadership in Education)** — a physical school of leadership modelled on college/university structures with Leadership Awards to recognise exemplary school leaders. But *"a physical institute like NILE would be insufficient … would take hundreds of years to reach all education leaders in the country and would struggle to remain contextually relevant given India's diversity."* Continuous deliberation evolved NILE into a population-scale mission → **ShikshaLokam (est. 2017).**

## SL's reimagined definition of leadership (verbatim)

> *"Leadership is the responsibility of anyone—be it a teacher, staff member, or community leader—who takes ownership of improving the school. We believe that leadership is an act of continuous improvement … continuously improving oneself and improving the context so that the opportunities for stakeholders around the individual improve."*

The **I HAVE / I CAN / I WISH** progression — load-bearing for SL's agency-restoration narrative:

> *"When provided with the necessary resources and support (which gives leaders a sense of **"I HAVE"**), they develop the knowledge and confidence to execute a set of improvements (**"I CAN"**) and with each such improvement carried out, they aspire to achieve more (**"I WISH"**)."*

## Global ambition (verbatim)

> *"While Shikshagraha begins its journey to impact 250 million children in India, its vision extends far beyond national borders … Globally, 260 million children are still out of school … the movement can offer solutions to educational crises in countries facing instability, inequality, and resource constraints. This global relevance highlights Shikshagraha's potential to not only elevate India's education system but also to serve as a blueprint for educational reform in other parts of the world, particularly in the Global South."*
