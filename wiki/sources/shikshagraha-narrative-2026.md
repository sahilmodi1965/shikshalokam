## What this doc holds

The canonical long-form narrative of the Shikshagraha movement. Sections in source order:

1. **A Crisis in Education — and a Moment to Transform** — sets the scale (1M+ public schools, 143M children) and names four challenge dimensions.
2. **Vision** — *"Every child of today's India, completes their education Grade 1-12, in an environment that values and enhances their potential — through a community-led, locally empowered, and continuously evolving public school system."*
3. **Why Public Schools Are Central to Educational Equity** — 200M children in public schools; transformation is "the only sustainable way" to ensure equity.
4. **The Challenge** — three named systemic challenges: Top-Down Tunnel Vision / Unempowered Leadership / Fragmented Efforts.
5. **Shikshagraha as Collective Aspiration** — Samaaj-first framing.
6. **People's Movement** — "mind and heart of the system."
7. **Satyagraha for Education** — five-row mapping of Indian Freedom Struggle indicators to Shikshagraha's approach.
8. ***Samaj* First Approach** — load-bearing four-actor framework: Samaaj / Sarkaar / Bazaar / Sanchar.
9. **Core Principles** — Collective Action / Decentralisation / Agency / Continuous Improvement.
10. **Roadmap for Exponential Change** — Local Problems Local Solutions / Leadership at Every Level / Leveraging Collective Strength.
11. **Implementation: Missions for Impact** — Systemic Leadership Collective / Community Transformation Mission (with Women + Youth Leadership Collectives).
12. **2030 Impact** — universal Grade 1–12 completion, FLN milestones, reduced transition dropout, community involvement targets.

## "As ShikshaLokam" — own-impact section (verbatim from source)

> **ACCESS:** Co-builders of DIKSHA (national education platform with Ministry of Education, Government of India). Shared digital infrastructure improving access to knowledge for leaders and teachers. Micro-improvement capability brings observability to how public schools are improving. **8 lakhs+ micro-improvements** led by school leaders and teachers.
>
> **NARRATIVE:** Helping create the narrative of the 'improving public schools.'
>
> **IMPACT:** Micro-improvement approach institutionalised at federal level + across states. Bihar uses MI approach for project-based learning across **30,000 middle schools reaching 60 lakhs children**. Third-party assessments show improvement on Science and life skills. Punjab — continuous improvement in student outcomes since 2021. Similar trajectories from Haryana, Nagaland, Andhra Pradesh.
>
> *"We have contributed to helping the system see better, solve better and sustain the change better."*
>
> Collective action narrative: since 2017, built ecosystem of **50+ civil society organisations**. Won Schwab Foundation award for collective social innovation at WEF 2023 via Punjab education collective. Created visibility for state governments' and CSOs' success stories on national stage.
