## Major challenges the school faced (verbatim summary)

1. **Network connectivity issues** — disrupted communication, online resources, on-time submission.
2. **Frequent power fluctuations** — irregular supply, smooth functioning of digital tasks disrupted.
3. **Delayed understanding among students** — many required additional time on project objectives;
   submission delays.
4. **First-generation learners** — most students; extra guidance needed for instructions, materials,
   independent task completion.
5. **Limited technical skills among teachers** — recording and editing videos a major hurdle.
6. **Socio-economic challenges of student families** — many parents are daily wage earners; students
   often need to assist family, especially during monsoon; attendance drops; project work delays.
7. **Lack of audio-visual learning resources** — no television for pre-primary; *the Head Teacher
   personally arranged a television from outside for classroom use* "so that students studying in
   government schools would not feel disadvantaged compared to others."
8. **Lack of proper classroom facilities** — limited space; group activities and interactive sessions
   difficult to structure.

## The micro-improvements (steps the school took)

**Literacy MIP** — *Ichaulu, Literacy Teacher.* Introduced a poem by reading aloud line by line with
proper pronunciation and explanation. Students recited together with her. Regular practice sessions
from Term 1 onwards, with **actions and expressions**. Increased participation, improved reading,
deepened poem comprehension.

**Numeracy MIP** — *Rebiheing, Numeracy Teacher.* Activities to identify different sizes of objects
and arrange objects in numerical order. Classroom materials and everyday objects used as
**activity-based learning**. Students learned to compare objects by size and weight — heaviest /
lightest, heavy / light — through hands-on practice.

**Pre-Primary MIP** — *Maheingaule, Pre-Primary Teacher.* Activity-based learning using locally
available materials — **pebbles, twigs, leaves, visuals, building blocks** — paired with **play-based
learning**. Children "find more enthusiastic and eager to learn compared to conventional method."

## Impact reported

- Literacy: students showed **noticeable improvement in reading, pronunciation, vocabulary,
  understanding**; more confident in reciting poems and reading stories with expressions and actions;
  classroom participation up; learning environment more interactive and engaging.
- Numeracy: better understanding of basic mathematical concepts; able to identify sizes and arrange
  objects in numerical order; more enthusiastic and active; improved participation and problem-solving.
- Pre-primary: students "became more active, confident, and eager to learn"; locally-available
  materials made learning enjoyable; improved communication, social interaction, creativity; more
  joyful and child-friendly classroom.

## The hardest moment

> *"Navigating the new application was challenging due to poor network connectivity and limited time
> for uploading videos. Uploading project videos often took a long time and understanding and
> navigating the app also required additional effort and practice. Despite these difficulties, we
> tried our best to complete and submit all project work on time."*

## How parents and community were brought along

> *"When the project was initially introduced, the school received many concerns and complaints from
> parents, as they were unfamiliar with the concept of project-based and creative learning. Since many
> parents were uneducated and unaware of these modern teaching methods, they found it difficult to
> understand the purpose and benefits of the activities. To address this issue, the school took the
> initiative to conduct Parent-Teacher Meetings to explain the objectives and importance of the project.
> These meetings helped parents gain a better understanding of the learning process and encouraged
> their support and involvement in their children's education."*

## Collaboration — in their own words

> *"Collaboration among teachers, students, parents, and the community played a vital role in the
> school's improvement journey. Teachers worked together to implement activity-based and project-based
> learning effectively, while students actively participated in classroom activities and projects.
> Parents were guided through Parent-Teacher Meetings to help them understand the importance of
> creative and experiential learning, which increased their support and involvement in their children's
> education. The community and school management also contributed by supporting learning resources and
> encouraging quality education. This collective effort created a positive learning environment,
> improved student participation and confidence, and helped in the overall development of the school."*

## What they will continue (post-Fest)

Activity-based learning · play-based learning · storytelling · poem recitation with actions · group
activities · locally-available teaching-learning materials · regular Parent-Teacher Meetings — *"because
they have helped students become more active, confident, creative and enthusiastic learners."*

## New practices introduced this year (beyond MIPs)

Activity-based and play-based classroom methods · pebbles, leaves, twigs, bottle caps, visuals,
charts, building blocks as TLMs for literacy and numeracy · storytelling and poem recitation with
actions · audio-visual learning via the borrowed television and educational videos.

## What now feels possible (the unlock)

> *"Before the Fest, the school felt that implementing activity-based and experiential learning
> effectively in a government school with limited resources would be very difficult. However, through
> continuous effort, collaboration, and innovative teaching practices, the school now strongly believes
> that **quality foundational learning is possible even with limited resources.** The school has
> realized that with creativity, teamwork and community support, students can become active, confident
> and enthusiastic learners."*

## On being recognised as a winning school

> *"Being recognised as a winning school by the Department of School Education is a matter of great
> pride and motivation for the teachers, students and the entire community. It reflects the hard work,
> dedication, and collective efforts of the school in improving the quality of education. This
> recognition has encouraged teachers to continue using innovative and child-centered teaching methods
> and has increased the confidence and trust of parents and the community towards the school."*

Forward plan: continue activity-based and play-based learning, strengthen literacy and numeracy
practices, conduct regular PTMs, make greater use of locally-available TLMs and audio-visual resources;
build a more inclusive, engaging, supportive learning environment.
