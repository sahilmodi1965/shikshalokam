# Stories for Brazil presentation — Lemann Foundation Summit

**Why this matters:** the freshest curated set of four collective-action case studies, prepared for the Lemann Foundation Summit (Brazil) — i.e. SL telling its story to a **Global South / non-India** audience. Useful both as content source (four crisp partner stories with collective composition + impact numbers) and as voice exemplar for international-audience framing.

## The four stories (canonical short summaries)

### 1. **Nagaland — Ensuring Future Readiness for Students (PBL)**

- **Objective:** Project-Based Learning (PBL) to develop problem-solving, critical thinking, 21st-century skills.
- **Challenge:** Only 27.1% of Grade 5 children in Nagaland government schools can read Grade 2-level text; 12.7% can do division (ASER 2024). State avg: 53% Language, 35% Maths (NAS 2021).
- **Approach:** Builds on PBL successes in Bihar, AP, Odisha. Embeds micro-improvements approach for Grades 4–5 English + Maths.
- **The Collective:**
  - **Department of School Education & Samagra Shiksha, Nagaland** — co-design, budget, implementation
  - **Education Above All** — content design + funding
  - **NagaED** — contextual program design + on-ground implementation
  - **Mantra4Change** — program design + capacity building of education leaders
  - **ShikshaLokam** — project management, government liaison, micro-improvements design, technology
- **Impact:** Pilot in 2 districts (Dimapur, Kiphire) — **400 teachers / 200 schools / 3,000 students.** Proposed scale: **11 districts, 1,500 schools, 2,500+ teachers, 17,800+ students.**
- **Collective action led to:** quicker orchestration; informed program design leveraging cross-state evidence.

### 2. **Punjab — Punjab Education Collective (PEC), Systemic Transformation**

- **Objective:** Transform Punjab's 19,000 public schools serving 13M+ children into spaces of learning, equity, future readiness.
- **Challenge:** Only 36% of Grade 3 could read Grade 2 text; half of Grade 5 struggled with basic math; 70% of 14–18-year-olds couldn't locate Punjab on a map.
- **Approach:** PEC drove systemic reform via co-creation with government, tech tools shaped by research+data+need, Samaaj-Sarkaar-Bazaar collective action.
- **The Collective:**
  - **Department of School Education, Punjab** — catalysing improvements across 19,000 schools
  - **Sanjhi Sikhya** — elementary education + teacher development
  - **Samarthya** — community engagement for school governance
  - **Mantra4Change** — leadership for thriving schools
  - **ShikshaLokam** — scaling leadership through open-source technology
  - **Deloitte, A.T.E. Chandra Foundation** — funding
  - **Azim Premji University** — capacity building
  - **McKinsey, Indian School of Development Management, Ashoka University** — advisory
- **Impact:** State **Performance Grading Index moved 13 (2019) → 1 (2020).** NAS 2021: Punjab ranked **#1.** Schwab Foundation **Collective Social Innovation Award.** Replicable trust-based models now inspiring Nagaland and Telangana collectives.
- **Collective action led to:** shared vision, state-wide adoption; system resilience through COVID-19; pooling of policy / pedagogy / tech / implementation expertise.

### 3. **Chhattisgarh — Facilitating School Readiness for India's Youngest Learners (ECE / Anganwadi)**

- **Objective:** Transform early childhood education in Anganwadi centres; enhance school readiness.
- **Challenge:** Anganwadi systems face limited frontline-worker agency, low community trust, poor infrastructure.
- **Approach:** Enhance organisation strategy + program design — baseline assessment, partnerships, capacity building for **CDPOs, supervisors, Anganwadi workers.**
- **The Collective:**
  - **Department of School Education, Chhattisgarh** — state-wide systematic adoption, policy integration
  - **Axis Bank** — funding
  - **EduWeave Foundation** — contextual intervention design + on-ground implementation
  - **Shikshagraha** — org development, intervention design + scale, strategic networks and funding access
- **Impact:** **73-point increase in school readiness in Raipur district.** Scale: **60 → 1,941 Anganwadis** in Raipur alone; further expansion in Jammu & Kashmir.

### 4. **Bihar — Generating Grassroots Demand for Education Equity (Shiksha Chaupals)**

- **Objective:** Make education a public agenda in Bihar — *"one that every citizen demands, and every leader prioritises."*
- **Challenge:** Bihar ranks last on UN SDG-4 100-indicator performance; lowest literacy rate in India.
- **Approach:** Enable parents, women, youth to **demand and support quality education** + equip teachers, school leaders, officials with micro-improvement tools.
- **The Collective:**
  - **Department of School Education + SCERT, Bihar** — institutionalisation across **75,000+ schools**
  - **Eight Women Federations** — facilitating **Shiksha Chaupals** (community gatherings to reimagine education delivery, co-design, dismantle barriers)
  - **Education Above All** — content design (lesson plans, PBL resources) + funding
  - **United Way of Delhi** — funding
  - **Mantra4Change** — project-based-learning implementation + leadership development
  - **ShikshaLokam** — scaling leadership through open-source technology
- **Impact:**
  - **55,000+ women + community leaders** in **1,100+ villages** at **2,450+ chaupals** enabled as co-creators of education change
  - **335 girls re-enrolled**; revival of **Meena Manch** (girl students' clubs for leadership development) in 97 schools across three blocks
  - In **28,000+ public schools** students made **up to two years' worth of learning in just one year**, especially Grade 7 & 8
- **Collective action led to:** quicker orchestration; leveraging existing evidence; joint state + funder commitment to institutionalise PBL.

## Distinctive framings worth noting

1. **"The Transformative Power of Collective Action: Evidence from the ground"** — the umbrella sub-title. Use when SL needs an evidentiary, partner-led framing (especially for global / non-India audiences).
2. **Each story names the full collective with role** — not just SL's contribution. This is the canonical "name everyone, credit the system" pattern; matches the Punjab Education Collective Schwab citation framing.
3. **ShikshaLokam's recurring role-words:** "project management, government liaison, micro-improvements design, application of technology" / "scaling leadership through open-source technology." Use these verbatim when describing SL's contribution in a multi-partner story.
4. **Number patterns:** state stat first (the challenge) → pilot/current numbers → proposed scale. Three-step ladder.

## How this brain uses it

When the daily user is drafting:
- A multi-partner collective-action story → use one of these four as the structural template (Objective / Challenge / Approach / Collective / Impact).
- A global-audience piece → use the verbatim Punjab Schwab line: *"The Schwab Foundation recognised the collective with the Collective Social Innovation Award."*
- Anything Bihar-Chaupal-related → the 55K women / 1,100 villages / 2,450 chaupals / 28K schools / 2-years-in-1-year stack lives here.
- ECE / Anganwadi content → Chhattisgarh story is the freshest data point (73-pt increase, 60→1,941 scale).
