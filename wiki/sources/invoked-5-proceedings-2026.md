# InvokED 5.0 Proceedings — what happened

Faithful digest of the InvokED 5.0 **summary** and **session transcripts** docs (both shared by
Sonal, 2026-06-01). Quotes reproduced as in source. Use as the proof-and-momentum bank for
[[../../projects/invoked-6/page]] — verified lines, outcomes, and stories 6.0 invites can cite to
show what last year's convening produced.

> Note: the summary doc truncates mid-heading at "Strategic Part…"; anything past that is not in
> source. A handful of speech-to-text artifacts in the transcripts are preserved as found.

## Structure

**Day 1 (6 Feb, plenary + Brain Dates + Tech Track):** Welcome Address (Kumari Shibulal, S.D.
Shibulal, Dr Urvashi Sahni; S.D. Shibulal keynote) → Fireside "Leadership in the New Age" (Wendy
Kopp, moderated by Vijayalakshmi Arumeni, Grade 11 student) → Shikshagraha Awards 2nd edition (jury
chair Dr Subramanian Rangan) → Reflections on Leadership (Dr Martin Kasper; Aradhana Dalmia) →
Leadership Spotlights (Jack Sim; Janhvi Maheshwari Kanoria; Keerti Jain / Amul) → Brain Date:
Evidence — Collectivizing Knowledge → Brain Date: Beyond Fundraising — Orchestrating Capital → Brain
Date: From Dialogue to Momentum (Alex Beard) → Panel: Painting a Vision for a Billion Futures
(Cynthia McCaffrey / Camila Pereira / Camille Massey / Archana Sharma Awasthi; moderated Meenakshi
Ramesh) → Tech Track: Impact Ignited, Lightning Talks, I4EE Hackathon Awards.

**Day 2 (7 Feb, Shikshagraha Utsav):** Sanjay Purohit address → SG Utsav panel (Shruti Shibulal;
Dr Kekhrielhoulie Yhome, Minister, Nagaland; Abhi Shah) → Keynote "Future Ready Children for a
Viksit Bharat" (Shri Anandrao V. Patil, Addl. Secretary, MoE) → **Bright Spots Showcase** (Tamil
Nadu / Mandya-Karnataka / Kalahandi-Odisha / Nagaland) → Brain Dates: Environmental Consciousness;
Student Agency & 21st-Century Skills; Inclusion ("In Their Shoes"); STEM & Responsible AI (closing
with launch of the **Shikshagraha STEM Collective**) → Closed-Door roundtables.

## Signature quotes (verbatim, attributed)

- **S.D. Shibulal:** "The leadership question of our time is this: how do we move from fragmented
  effort to shared responsibility?" · "No single actor or sector can transform education on its own."
- **Wendy Kopp:** "Leadership in the new age is not about being the smartest or most powerful person
  in the room—it is about listening deeply, sharing responsibility, and enabling collective wisdom
  to drive change." · "My biggest hope is that many young people like you decide to begin your
  careers in close proximity to the biggest inequities and challenges our communities face."
- **Dr Subramanian Rangan:** "Leaders make decisions, but ownership is about choices. Decisions may
  be about information, but choices are about identity — about who we are." · "Now, perhaps we will
  value the best questions and the most curiosity — because AI will support knowledge, but curiosity
  drives learning."
- **Dr Martin Kasper:** "It is not about bricks and mortar, and not about individuals—it is about
  inspiring activities that connect communities and move many people to act together." · "The problem
  is not a lack of ideas or skilled individuals — it is the fragmentation of efforts." · "I look
  forward to the day when I come simply as a friend and not as a benefactor."
- **Jack Sim (World Toilet Organization):** "If you want to solve global problems, working alone has
  limits, but working with others creates unlimited possibilities."
- **Cynthia McCaffrey (UNICEF India):** "We all have a responsibility to find a way to do it now." ·
  recounting "Hina": **"We don't want charity, we want a chance."**
- **Camila Pereira (Global School Leaders):** "We need to start seeing school leaders not as the last
  mile of reforms [but as] an essential part of any reform."
- **Camille Massey (Synergos):** "When people interact directly and build relationships over time,
  the power dynamics begin to shift."
- **Nita Agarwal (Co-Impact):** "Trust is the currency of action."
- **Sanjay Purohit:** "What is the one thing that I will do today to improve public schools in India?
  Do one small thing… then you're already a part of Shikshagraha." · "Never build again, always build
  beyond. Compounded interest is always better than simple interest." · "This movement calls you to
  be very stubborn about seeking excellence in education for all and equity in education for all."
- **Dr Kekhrielhoulie Yhome (Minister, Nagaland):** "It's too dangerous to leave [education] to
  government alone… If we come together for a shared goal… we can turn these micro miracles into
  something magical."
- **Manju Balasubramanyam (DPS Bangalore North):** "Diversity is being invited to the party,
  inclusion is being asked to dance" — adding equity is "a dance floor where I can dance."

## Recurring themes

Collective leadership over individual heroism · fragmentation is the enemy · Samaaj–Sarkaar–Bazaar
(–Sanchar) coming together · agency & ownership at every level (student → teacher → school leader →
ministry) · **micro-improvement** (small steps that compound) · AI/technology as enabler not silver
bullet ("a thinking companion, not an answer engine") · holistic / future-ready education · the
urgency of the Global South & one billion futures · capital beyond money (trust, networks,
relationships) · build-on-what-exists ("never reinvent the wheel"; Shikshagraha Commons).

## Outcomes / announcements (cite-able momentum for 6.0)

- **Shikshagraha Awards 2026 (2nd edition):** presented to three grassroots leaders; top-10 stories
  published as the coffee-table book *"Small Steps to Build Great Schools — Stories of Shikshagraha
  Awards 2026,"* launched at the event.
- **I4EE Hackathon 2026:** 2,800+ participants, 165+ solutions.
- **Movement dashboard (Purohit, event morning):** ~**1.2 million micro-improvements**, ~**343,000
  leaders** driving improvement.
- **Shikshagraha Commons** launched — open, multilingual repository of practices.
- **Shikshagraha STEM Collective** launched — pan-India STEM collective anchored by Maker Ghat.
- **Bright-spot scale numbers:** Tamil Nadu House System now in **37,500 schools** (goal: 1M student
  leaders by 2030); Tirunelveli enrolment **75% → 95.8%**; Nagaland NLNF 3.0 = **22,000
  micro-improvement programs** (up from 13,000 in 2.0); Odisha/Kalahandi scaled 15 schools → full
  district.
- **MoE updates (Anandrao Patil):** *Vidya Pravesh* reached 30M+ children; APAAR IDs ~15.6 crore;
  Diksha 2.0 launched; AI Centres of Excellence incl. AICOE for Education (IIT Madras); PARAKH
  Holistic Progress Card adopted by ~18 states.
- **Nagaland (Minister Yhome):** committed to formally join hands with Mantra4Change + ShikshaLokam.

## Memorable grassroots stories (named, for narrative pull)

Award winners — *Nellore Subramaniam* (Science on Wheels, rural AP); *Kumari Bharti / Gutti*
(menstrual-hygiene + anti-child-marriage work, Bihar); *Batskhem Taba* (a "Living Library" of local
wisdom, Meghalaya). Plus: *Hina* (UNICEF — orphaned, caught by India's systems, now in a bank);
Mandya's *Rani Chandrashekar* + BEO *Raghu* ("when Samaj and Sarkar work together, listening becomes
the foundation for equity"); Tamil Nadu's *Regila Marinus* (House System) + DRO *M. Suganya*
(enrolment 75% → 95.8%); Nagaland's *Kevisato Sanyu* + Dy. Mission Director *Kelhikha Kenye* ("scale
not driven by mandates, but by meaning").
