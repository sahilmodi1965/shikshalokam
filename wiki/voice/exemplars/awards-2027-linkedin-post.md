## Anatomy — the campaign-announcement pattern

| Move | What this post does | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| **Opening headline** | "Shikshagraha Awards 2027 nominations are now LIVE!" — single sentence, exclamation, ALL-CAPS energy word ("LIVE") | Action-oriented, urgent, scannable in feed |
| **Institutional credit** | "instituted by The Shibulal Family Philanthropic Initiatives (SFPI)" — credit upfront, in full, with parenthetical abbreviation | Funder visibility + transparency in the same breath |
| **Purpose sentence** | "celebrate exceptional education leaders who go beyond their roles to create a more equitable and inclusive public education system" | Names the *type* of leader before any individual. Movement-level framing. |
| **Three-line audience frame** | Stacked short lines: *"Women leading communities. Youth igniting change. Teachers and school leaders building the future from within the system."* | Maps directly to the three Leadership Collectives — Women, Youth, Systemic. Reads as a rhythm, not a list. |
| **Direct ask** | "Know such a leader? Nominate them and honour their contribution:" — colon ending invites the link block | Personal, second-person, soft CTA |
| **Emoji-led link block** | 🔗 prefix + bulleted languages | Scannable in mobile feeds; multilingual is the headline of the link block |
| **Multilingual ladder** | English / Hindi / Kannada / Tamil | Signals national outreach + south-India equity (Tamil + Kannada are deliberate) |
| **Learn-more link** | Separate line, no emoji clutter | Funnels curious-but-not-ready readers |
| **Hashtag stack** | 4 hashtags: campaign-specific + movement + theme + cross-cutting | Standard ShikshaLokam structure |
| **Tag list** | Team members tagged in comments / post body | Cross-promotion within network |

## Voice rules this exemplifies

- ALWAYS: institutional sponsor named in full + abbreviation
- ALWAYS: stacked short lines for "who we celebrate" — readable as a rhythm
- ALWAYS: multilingual link block when the call is national in scope
- ALWAYS: campaign-specific hashtag first (#ShikshagrahaAwards2027), then movement, then theme, then cross-cutting
- NEVER: passive voice ("nominations are now live" — present + active)
- NEVER: skipping institutional credit (SFPI) even in short-form
- NEVER: only English when the call is national
