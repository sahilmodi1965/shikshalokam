# SM Captions 2026-27 — live captions voice (Sonal-authored)

The current year's working captions doc. Sonal drafts here weekly; the brain pulls voice patterns from it. **Refresh exemplar at every monthly enrichment pass — this doc grows.**

## Monthly weekday rhythm (canonical scheduling cadence)

| Weekday | Post type |
|---|---|
| Monday | Feedback video |
| Tuesday | Tech (Hackathon, MItra) \| Research Insight |
| Wednesday | InvokED Studio teaser |
| Thursday | Buffer OR Ed-leader portrait |
| Friday | InvokED Session Post (Teaser / Carousel / Quote) |

When Sonal asks "draft Tuesday's research insight post," the brain respects this rhythm. Posts on the wrong day of the week violate convention.

## April 2026 exemplars — real captions from the doc

### InvokED session teaser (April 1) — opening move + named conversation

> Wendy Kopp (Co-founder & CEO, Teach For All), in this conversation with Vijaylaxmi Aramani (Student, The Samhita Academy), reflect on how our understanding of leadership is evolving in today's world.
>
> They explore how leadership is no longer about position or authority, but about people coming together — taking responsibility, learning from one another, and working toward a shared purpose. The conversation highlights the shift from individual leadership to collective leadership, where both students and adults see themselves as active participants in change.
>
> ▶️ Watch the full session on YouTube: https://youtu.be/o5sB-vA93NU
>
> #InvokED2026 #Leadership #MissionToMovement #CollectiveLeadership
>
> Share your thoughts with us in the comments below 👇!

**Voice rules this exemplifies:**
- Named conversation participants with org + role in parentheses
- Opens with "in this conversation with" — no buildup
- ▶️ emoji for video link (canonical YouTube indicator)
- 4-hashtag stack (event + theme + cross-cutting + specific)
- Closing CTA: "Share your thoughts with us in the comments below 👇!"

### Research insight (Tuesday, May 5) — IDR article framing

> What if the problem with India's education system isn't a lack of effort, but a shortage of leaders at every level?
>
> In this piece for @IDR, @Shaheen Mistri (Founder & CEO of @Teach For India), makes the case that educational inequity is too complex for any one person or organisation to solve. What it demands is a movement: children who can voice their needs, teachers who have real agency, and institutions that build the conditions for both to thrive.
>
> This is the movement Shikshalokam is part of — growing leaders across every level of the education ecosystem, together.
>
> Swipe through for the key ideas. 👇
>
> 📍 Full article in comments.

**Voice rules:**
- Open with a sharp question (the "what if" form)
- Cite the author + outlet + their role
- One paragraph distilling the argument
- Connect to ShikshaLokam's positioning explicitly ("This is the movement Shikshalokam is part of")
- 👇 for swipe / scroll
- 📍 for "in comments" indicator

## How `shikshalokam-write` uses this

- Match the weekday rhythm if context suggests one

- Apply emoji scaffolding (▶️ video, 📍 detail, 📌 register, 👇 swipe, 🔴 live)
- Tag partners with @
- Close with the appropriate CTA + hashtag stack
