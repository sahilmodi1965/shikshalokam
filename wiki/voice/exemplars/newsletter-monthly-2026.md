# Monthly newsletter — anatomy + voice (2026)

The canonical shape for ShikshaLokam's monthly newsletter: a warm first-person opener, then
~5 short "story" blocks, each a headline + 1–2 tight context paragraphs + one CTA link. Tone is
literary, understated, anti-hero — data always paired with a human consequence. Provided by the
team 2026-06-17 as the reference for this month's drafts; treat as user-sourced.

## Overall anatomy (top → bottom)

1. **Personalised greeting** — "Dear <Name>," + a one-line warm check-in ("Hope this message finds you in good spirits.")
2. **Thematic opener (the soul of the issue)** — one reflective paragraph that names a truth about
   change, then cascades into a list of human vignettes standing in for the four actors
   (Samaaj/Sarkaar/Bazaar/Sanchar) without naming the framework:
   > "a mother in Bihar who decides that her child's education comes before any other responsibility.
   > A teacher in Nagaland who listens to her students with intent & empathy. A student whose learning
   > journey doesn't just consist of listening but also asking the relevant questions. A social
   > entrepreneur creating a shared space for collective meaning-making. A philanthropist who invests
   > not just funds, but time in people."
   - Closes with the framing line + the "window into the movement" move:
   > "This is what Shikshagraha looks like in action — across chaupals, classrooms, and convenings.
   > This edition is yet another window into the movement, in practice, across India."
3. **"Happy reading..."** — single line, then the story blocks.
4. **~5 story blocks** (see shape below).
5. **Footer** — "Follow us!" + LinkedIn / YouTube / Website / Spotify / Twitter, feedback line
   (`social@shikshalokam.org`), unsubscribe ("reply with a 'U'").

## The story block (repeat ~5×)

- **Headline = evocative phrase : specific subtitle.** The first half is a small thesis; the second
  half grounds it. Examples from the reference:
  - *"The Pledge That Became a Practice: From Bihar's Chaupals to Los Angeles"*
  - *"Shiksha Samvaad: Building the Bridge Between Intent and Practice"*
  - *"A State That Chose to Keep Going: Thousands of Improvements and the Schools Behind Them"*
  - *"Up Close & Personal with GenNext: What Happens When Students Ask The Questions"*
  - *"The People Behind the Classrooms: Stories of Educators and Communities Driving Change"*
- **Body = 1–2 short paragraphs (~50–100 words).** Lead with the human/insight, not the logistics.
  Numbers arrive mid-paragraph and always carry meaning ("1500+ MIPs. That number alone tells a
  story."). Reframe achievement as culture, not contest: *"less a competition and more a culture."*
- **One CTA line per block** — imperative + destination: "Watch 'Mothers of Courage' on YouTube",
  "Read the Micro-improvement Compendium here", "Read the official winner announcement here".

## Voice rules this exemplifies

- **Change is incremental and quiet** — open on the small step, not the milestone ("change in public
  education rarely arrives all at once... every small, at times quiet step").
- **Anti-hero, system-wide** — credit lands on communities, teachers, schools collectively; partners
  are named (Elevate Foundation, Sol's ARC, Peepul, Bachpan Manao, Teach For All, Synergos…).
- **Insight before logistics** — each block earns the link by saying something true first.
- **House vocabulary** — "Shikshagraha in action," "micro-improvement(s) / MIPs," "chaupals,
  classrooms, and convenings," "collective meaning-making," "movement, in practice."
- **Em-dash + comma cadence** for the reflective opener; short declaratives inside the blocks.
- **No superlatives, no urgency tropes** — the recognition (LA Film Awards semi-final, NLNF wins) is
  reported plainly and turned back toward the people behind it.

## Issue conventions (sent via YAMM mail-merge)

- **Greeting:** keep the literal merge tag `Dear <<First Name>>,` — YAMM fills it per recipient.
- **CTAs:** real links are supplied per issue; use clear placeholders until then.
- **Sign-off:** *Khushboo Awasthi · Chief Curator, Shikshagraha.* ⚠️ The Chief Curator title is
  flagged confidential in the brain ([[khushboo-designation-confidential]]) — confirm clearance
  before it goes out externally each time.
