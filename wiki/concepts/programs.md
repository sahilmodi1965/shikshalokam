# Programs

Named initiatives ShikshaLokam runs or convenes.

## Active

### Shikshagraha
The people's movement; ShikshaLokam catalyses, does not own. "A people's movement to improve India's 1 million public schools." Anchored in Sense → Make Sense → Learn → Improve. Restores agency of system leaders, communities, youth, women.
Source: [shikshalokam.org](https://shikshalokam.org/)

### InvokED — A Global Dialogue on Education Leadership
Annual convening.
- **InvokED 2026:** held 6–7 February 2026 at Prestige Centre for Performing Arts (PCPA), Bengaluru. Speakers included SD Shibulal (SFPI / ShikshaLokam), Wendy Kopp (Teach For All), Martin Kasper (Childaid Network Foundation), Shabnam Sinha. Co-organised with Shikshagraha and Mantra4Change.
- **InvokED 2027 (next):** 26–27 February 2027 (per homepage reference).

Sources: [thewire.in](https://m.thewire.in/article/ptiprnews/invoked-2026-brings-global-and-grassroots-leaders-together-as-shikshagraha-announces-2026-awardees), [shikshalokam.org](https://shikshalokam.org/), [shikshalokam.org/invoked](https://shikshalokam.org/invoked/)

### ELEVATE
Technology platform: **Energising Leadership with Enhanced Visibility and Administrative Transformation towards Excellence.** Open-source microservices for observation, mentoring, analysis, improvement projects.
- Built on **Sunbird** (EkStep Foundation's open-source building blocks)
- All new capabilities contributed back to Sunbird as OSS components
- Hosted on / interoperable with **DIKSHA** (national platform; same Sunbird stack)

Source: [shikshalokam.org](https://shikshalokam.org/), search-derived material from Sunbird / DIKSHA documentation.

### Nagaland Literacy & Numeracy Fest (NLNF) 3.0 — feeding NIPUN Nagaland
State improvement programme inside Nagaland's drive to **NIPUN Nagaland — foundational literacy and numeracy
for all by 2027**. Built on continuous micro-improvements and active community collaboration; deepening and
broadening its reach across schools every cycle. Structure (per Sonal's framing brief, 2026-05-26):

- **Pre-Primary to Grade 3** — five Micro-Improvement Project (MIP) cycles around foundational skills.
- **Grades 4 and 5** — introduced to **Project-Based Learning (PBL)** in language and mathematics —
  critical thinking, problem-solving, real-world application. This is the strand the **DEP / Nagaland PBL
  macro story** sits inside (400 teachers, 200 schools across Dimapur and Kiphire) — see
  [[shikshalokam-brazil-stories-may2026]] § Nagaland for the Brazil-presentation framing.
- **Structured mentoring layer** — workshops, online sessions, one-to-one guidance for teachers and
  school leaders.
- **Mega PTMs across all grades** — parents and communities as active partners.
- **Recognition stack** — school-, district-, state-level celebration of teacher, student, parent
  contributions.
- **Governance + evaluation** — to keep the initiative accountable and scalable.

**The 15-winning-schools coffee table book** (in production, 2026): celebrates 15 NLNF 3.0 winning schools
under the themes of **collective effort** + **micro-improvement** (series of small steps to achieve an
objective). First winning-school response ingested:
[[nlnf3-gms-jalukie-sectorb-response-2026-05-07]] (GMS Jalukie Sector-B, Peren). Coffee-book live workspace:
[[../../projects/nagaland-coffee-book/page]].

**The collective** behind NLNF 3.0 (per Sonal's brief + Brazil-presentation cross-reference):
Department of School Education + Samagra Shiksha Nagaland + SCERT Nagaland · ShikshaLokam (project
management, micro-improvements design, government liaison, technology) · Mantra4Change · NagaED ·
Education Above All (content + funding).

Sources: [[nlnf3-gms-jalukie-sectorb-response-2026-05-07]], [[shikshalokam-brazil-stories-may2026]] § Nagaland.
