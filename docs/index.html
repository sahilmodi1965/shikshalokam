What I know about us

Our impact, in our own words.

From the canonical Shikshagraha Narrative (Drive). The four pillars of ShikshaLokam's contribution to the movement — distinct from any partner organisation's.

Access

DIKSHA — the national platform.

Co-builders of DIKSHA — the national education platform of the Ministry of Education, Government of India. Shared digital infrastructure improving access to knowledge for leaders and teachers across India's public schools.

Narrative

The story of improving public schools.

We help build the public story of improving government schools — not as a problem to be solved, but as places of hope and ambition. This is sustained through Shiksha Samvaads, Shiksha Safars, InvokED, and the published archive of leader portraits and campaign stories.

Impact

Institutionalised at federal + state level.

Micro-improvement approach now embedded at federal level + across multiple states. Bihar runs project-based learning across 30,000 middle schools reaching 60 lakh children. Punjab — continuous improvement in student outcomes since 2021. Similar trajectories in Haryana, Nagaland, Andhra Pradesh.

Ecosystem

50+ civil society organisations.

Built and convened since 2017. Won the Schwab Foundation award for collective social innovation at the World Economic Forum 2023 (via the Punjab education collective). Created national visibility for state governments' and CSO partners' success stories.

How we sound

Voice — from Khushboo's pen.

The canonical ShikshaLokam stakeholder voice lives in Khushboo Awasthi's signed newsletters and in the live SM Captions doc Sonal maintains. Both are in the brain.

Always

  • Human story first, data second — name a leader before any statistic
  • Hopeful but honest — name the problem, then name the resilience
  • Present-tense urgency — "we must continue"
  • Collective frame — "we," not "I"
  • Three-line triplet sign-off — Together, we lead. Together, we inspire. Together, we achieve education equity.
  • Signature tagline — har kadam shiksha ki oar..! (every step towards education)

Never

  • Passive voice
  • Jargon dropped without earning it ("scalable solutions" only after concreteness)
  • Condescension toward government schools or communities
  • Inflated or unverified impact claims
  • Cross-brand transplants — partner organisations are named + sourced, never blurred
"We are the stories we tell. And the year has been all about stories that inspire. From mothers pledging for their daughters' education, to teachers visiting homes to bring girls back to school, and youth advocating for young children, communities have stood tall in championing the right to education. Every story of change reminds us why Shikshagraha began and why we must continue." — Khushboo Awasthi, Co-Founder & COO, ShikshaLokam. Year-end newsletter, January 2024. Movement frame

Samaaj-first — Shikshagraha's core architecture.

At the heart of Shikshagraha lies a Samaaj-first framework — a way of working where civil society takes the centre, and the government, markets, and media align in service. ShikshaLokam builds and sustains this architecture across the movement.

Samaaj

Civil society

At the centre — the catalyst. Students, parents, teachers, local communities driving change from the ground up.

Sarkaar

Government

Shapes responsive policy, empowering communities as active partners in decision-making.

Bazaar

Markets

Provides innovation, technology, and resources — supporting localised solutions.

Sanchar

Narrative

Amplifies voices, celebrates success stories, fosters a shared vision for quality education across all levels. This brain is one expression of Sanchar.
