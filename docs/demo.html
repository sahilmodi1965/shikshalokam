Two ways to use me

Brainstorm with me, or send Sahil a brief.

Both yield the same: a live URL the team can review. They differ only in who's driving — and what kind of brief you're starting from.

💬

You open Claude Code yourself

Brainstorm-led · in your own session
For the day-to-day rhythm — captions, edits, asking what we already say. You think out loud; I edit the right project page in place; we both watch it fill up.
  • Quick captions on Tuesday or Friday
  • A tweak on an existing draft
  • "What do we say about Punjab?" / "Remind me about Bihar Chaupals"
  • Small one-paste briefs that don't need heavy reading

What I do: read your brief, route to the right workspace, draft in place, push, hand back the URL.

📨

You email Sahil a brief

Directed run · Sahil routes
For briefs that arrive with source material attached — interview transcripts, PDFs, Google Sheets, Drive folders. Anything that needs heavy reading. Sahil runs the session; you get a review URL.
  • An ed-leader portrait from an interview transcript
  • A coffee-book story from a winning-school form sheet
  • A repurpose of a partner-shared deck
  • Any "here's the source, please draft" ask

What he does: ingests sources, drafts in our voice, mirrors to the live site, sends you the URL.

How I learn — a real example from this week

Every "actually, no" becomes a rule I keep.

Here's a real correction Sonal made on 26 May — and how it became a permanent rule the brain now uses for every future Shikshagraha mention. This is the loop that makes me sharper, week on week.

⛔ Before — my first draft

"Shiksha Chaupals are community gatherings — brought to life by Shikshagraha — where women, parents, and local leaders come together…"

↓
💬 Sonal — three rounds of feedback

"Shikshagraha is the movement. It bringing the gatherings to life feels shallow. It's the efforts of communities, women of self-help groups, our partners like Jyoti Mahila Samakhya, Srishti Mahila Samakhya, didis, parents who are bringing this to life — all happening under the movement."

↓
✅ After — approved by Sonal

"Shiksha Chaupals are community gatherings at the heart of the Shikshagraha movement — where women from self-help groups, didis who take the lead, parents, and partners like Jyoti Mahila Samakhya, Srishti Mahila Samakhya, and other SHGs and CSOs come together to sense what their nearest public schools need and act on it."

✨ Saved as a permanent rule — "Movement as frame, people as actors." Every future draft mentioning Shikshagraha now leads with the movement as the container, then names the real people inside it. The rule is filed in my voice styleguide. Sonal doesn't have to give the same correction twice.
