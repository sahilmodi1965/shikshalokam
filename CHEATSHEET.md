# Talking to the brain — the cheat-sheet

You never touch git, files, or commands. You just talk. Copy any line below into the brain
(Claude Code, inside the `shikshalokam` folder) and go. Everything saves + publishes for you.

## Warm up (do these first)
- `What do we know about Shikshagraha and our impact numbers?`
- `Here's a piece I'm proud of — remember it as a voice example.` *(paste your best writing — this is the single biggest thing you can give the brain; it learns how you sound)*

## Make content
- `Draft a LinkedIn post about <a leader / impact story / milestone>.`
- `Write the invite for <event>.`
- `Draft captions for <campaign>.`
- `Continue the <project> page` *(e.g. a leader portrait)*.
- `This isn't quite our voice — too corporate, make it warmer.` *(it fixes the draft and updates what it knows, same session)*

## Email & calendar (your Google, gated)
First time only: `Set me up for Google` *(one browser click — then you're done forever)*.
- `Draft an email to <person> about <topic>.` → lands in **your** Gmail Drafts.
- Review it, then `Send it.` → nothing leaves without that explicit yes.
- `Set up a 30-min call with <person> Thursday 3pm.`
- `Send the invite.` → attendees are emailed only when you say so.

## How content gets approved (the workflow)
- Anyone drafts → it lives **in the brain**, versioned + visible (the GitHub brain is our single source of truth).
- **Sonal approves explicitly** → only then does the brain write the approved version to the shared Google Drive folder.
- Sonal: `This is approved — publish it to Drive as a Doc.`

## Ask the brain anything
- `What have we said about <topic>?`
- `Remind me about <person / partner / project>.`
- `Here's stuff I found: <paste / link>. Add it to the brain.`

## The house style
The brain talks back **short, structured, managed-upwards** — a glance tells you what to do next.
The *content it writes for you* gets full length + full voice; only how it *talks about* the work stays brief.
