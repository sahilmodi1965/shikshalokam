# Voice

How ShikshaLokam sounds in its own materials. Pulled from the homepage, welcomeaboard subdomain, and a long-form blog post by Khushboo Awasthi (Co-Founder & COO).

## Distinctive register

**Aspirational, but pragmatic.** Refuses heroism. Foregrounds incremental, unglamorous work as the actual unit of leadership.

> Leadership Isn't About Titles – It's About the Act of Improvement
— [shikshalokam.org](https://shikshalokam.org/)

> Every School Transformation Story Begins with a Leader
— [shikshalokam.org](https://shikshalokam.org/)

> small, incremental changes or improvements...often made quickly with minimal resources
— [welcomeaboard.shikshalokam.org](https://welcomeaboard.shikshalokam.org/)

**Inclusive "we"; rhetorical questions.** Talks to readers as fellow practitioners, not subjects.

> Do you know of a great school without an able and efficient leader? Impossible!
— [welcomeaboard.shikshalokam.org](https://welcomeaboard.shikshalokam.org/)

**Conversational openings; everyday metaphor.** A long blog post by the COO compares leadership to "living in Goa" — sustained metaphor, conversational, unafraid of the mundane.

> Leadership roles are enticing... we often have a romanticized view of what it entails... leadership also involves handling unglamorous responsibilities such as performance reviews
— Khushboo Awasthi, [Leading Is Like Living In Goa](https://shikshalokam.org/leading-is-like-living-in-goa/)

> Just as living in Goa requires resilience and adaptability, so does leadership
— Khushboo Awasthi, same piece

## Recurring vocabulary

- **"Leaders" / "leadership"** — load-bearing words; almost always pluralised, almost always inclusive of system, community, youth, women
- **"Micro-improvements"** — house term; signals theory of change
- **"Ecosystem"** — used non-technically, to mean the field of actors
- **"People-Powered Movement"** / "people's movement" — Shikshagraha framing
- **"Sense, Make Sense, Learn, Improve"** — operating cycle, used as titling
- **"Catalyst"** — self-description (Education Leadership Catalyst)

## What it avoids

- Top-down language ("mandate," "rollout," "deliver to")
- Hero narratives (one teacher / one school as exception)
- Technocratic platform-talk — ELEVATE/DIKSHA are framed by what leaders do with them, not the tech itself
